Myron Rolle

Myron Rolle was a Florida State football player who played at the university starting in January of 2006. Before coming to Florida State he attended the Hun School of Princeton and was a nationally ranked football player even in high school.

However Myron was unlike many other college players during his college days. In addition to having the aspiration to reach the NFL, Myron also had plans of becoming a neurosurgeon after he graduated.

Some facts about myron rolle

  • He was the top college football safety prospect coming out of college
  • He received around 80 scholarship offers to play football at various divison I colleges
  • In college he majored in exercise science on a premedical track
  • He graduated from Florida State in two and a half years
  • He obtained the Rhodes Scholarship which is only given to 80 students in the entire United States
  • At Oxford he studied Medical Anthropology, receiving a masters of science in the subject
  • While at oxford he trained for the NFL draft because he would enter the draft after returning to the states. (Here's a video going into Myron's time at Oxford)
  • When he joined the NFL draft he was signed to the Tennessee Titans. He played for them for two years then moved over to the Steelers for a year
  • After his time in the NFL he moved on to his medical studies
  • He is currently starting his residency at Harvard Medical School
